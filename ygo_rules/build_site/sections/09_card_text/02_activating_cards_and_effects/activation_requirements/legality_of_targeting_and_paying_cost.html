You cannot choose to use a card or effect that targets unless there is already a card for it to target[1].

  • If, after paying the cost of that card or effect, a new target appears that didn't exist before, you can choose that card as the target of your new effect[1][2].
  • Even if paying a cost of the card or effect you want to activate would create a target for it, you cannot activate it unless the required number of targets already exist[1][2].
    Example Question:
    Can I Special Summon the Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster I discarded as a cost to activate the effect of "Lumina, Lightsworn Summoner" with that effect?
    Also, if it is possible, can I activate the effect if there currently is no Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster in my Graveyard that can be Special Summoned?
    Answer:
    You can Special Summon the Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster you discarded as a cost to activate the effect of "Lumina, Lightsworn Summoner" with that effect.
    It cannot be activated if there is no Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster currently in your Graveyard.
  • You control a card with an effect has multiple effects that you can choose (bulleted effects with "●") after paying a cost and the cost is the same. If:
    • The targets for one of the effects, "● effect 1", exist in the correct location, the card's effect can be legally activated and you can choose to resolve "● effect 1"
    • The targets for another one of the effects, "● effect 2", don't exist in the correct location, the card's effect can still be legally activated but you can't choose to resolve "● effect 2"
    But if after paying the cost of that card or effect, a new target appears that didn't exist before that is a target(s) for "● effect 2", and now "● effect 2" has all its targets now, "● effect 2" can be chosen at this time[2].
    Example Question:
    Ⓐ: If there are only two monsters in my Graveyard, including at least one non-Effect Monster, and a monster is attached to "Daigusto Emeral" as a material, can I activate "Daigusto Emeral"'s "target 3 monsters in your Graveyard; shuffle all 3 into the Deck, then draw 1 card" effect by detaching that monster?
    Ⓑ: If there are three Effect Monsters in my Graveyard, and a non-Effect Monster is attached to "Daigusto Emeral" as material, can I activate "Daigusto Emeral"'s "target 1 non-Effect Monster in your Graveyard; Special Summon that target" effect by detaching that non-Effect Monster?
    Answer:
    Yes, you can.
    You can activate "Daigusto Emeral"'s first effect as long as you can currently resolve at least one of the ● bulleted effects. Therefore, even if there are only two monsters in your Graveyard, as long as one of them is a non-Effect Monster that could be targeted by the second ● bulleted effect, you can activate "Daigusto Emeral"'s first effect.
    If you do so, and the card detached as a cost is a Monster Card which is sent to the Graveyard as a result, you can then still choose the first ● bulleted effect as the effect to activate. (This also applies to activating the second ● bulleted effect.)
    Finally, note that if there are two or fewer monsters in your Graveyard, and none of them are non-Effect Monsters, "Daigusto Emeral"'s first effect cannot be activated.
